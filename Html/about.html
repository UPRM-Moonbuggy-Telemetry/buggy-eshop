History

The UPRM Moonbuggy Project Design Team was founded in 2012 at the University of Puerto Rico at Mayag√ºez by a group of students from Mechanical Engineering. Since then we have brought together students from a wide variety of engineering departments, like Electrical, Software, Computer, Civil, and Industrial Engineering. Our objective is to manufacture a lightweight, strong, and cost effective human-powered space exploration vehicle. The team participates in the NASA'S Human Exploration Rover Challenge. which is held every year in the "U.S. Space and Rocke Center" in Huntsville, Alabama. The team members must design a vehicle that can withstand a series of engineering problems, similar to the problems encountered by the original Apollo 11 team. Each rover must be powered by two students (one male and one female) on a track that simulates lunar terrain including craters, rocks, slopes and lunar traps.

Mision

Design and manufacture a lightweight, strong, and cost effective human-powered space exploration vehicle, in order to develop as future engineers through a real-life situation.

Vision

One of the teams main goals is to develop students, creating the successors and leaders for the upcoming years. The team offers dynamic workshops, hands-on tasks, presentations and management roles to teach and develop the engineers of tomorrow.
